Where data science meets full-stack engineering

I'm a data scientist turned full-stack developer, currently building the clinical data-review platforms that biometrics and clinical teams rely on to monitor patient safety and data quality across trials. My day-to-day spans the whole stack — interactive R/Shiny visualizations, a React/Next.js admin dashboard, and the PostgreSQL schema that ties them together.

Before this, I trained at Johns Hopkins University in environmental health engineering, where I led real-world evidence research on electronic health records (TriNetX, NHIRD) and built decision-support dashboards. That background — rigorous statistics, large messy datasets, and a healthcare domain — is exactly what I now bring to clinical software.

I care about clean, reviewable code, performance on large datasets, and shipping tools people actually use. I work to CDISC standards and clinical-grade quality expectations.
